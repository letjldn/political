# Design Paper

## Core Idea

This is not a traditional political quiz.

The aim is to identify what users actually want from society before asking which party best represents them.

The questionnaire should separate:

1. Desired outcomes
2. Root-cause beliefs
3. Acceptable trade-offs
4. Trust in institutions
5. Policy mechanisms
6. Party alignment

## Design Principle

People often vote based on slogans, fear, identity, party loyalty or media framing.

This tool should avoid asking obvious party-coded questions.

Instead of asking:

> Should immigration be reduced?

Ask questions that reveal what the user actually wants:

- safer streets
- cheaper housing
- higher wages
- stronger communities
- more cultural continuity
- better public services

The result should then explain which political platforms most closely match those underlying priorities.

## Tone

Questions should be:

- simple
- neutral
- short
- non-judgemental
- non-academic
- not obviously left or right coded

A regular person should understand every question instantly.

## Main Hidden Dimensions

The engine should score users across hidden dimensions including:

- economic fairness
- public investment
- personal freedom
- law and order
- national identity
- immigration openness
- cultural tradition
- social progress
- climate priority
- housing affordability
- democratic reform
- anti-corruption
- media trust
- privacy
- technology control
- religious influence
- international cooperation
- institutional trust
- personal responsibility
- structural responsibility

## Output

The final result should include:

- best party match
- top four party matches
- percentage scores
- strongest values
- main tensions
- explanation of why the result happened
- explanation of why other parties scored lower
- disclaimer
